How violent
is the corpus?

Each image was scored on implied violence from 0 (none) to 10 (maximum). The distribution reveals a striking bimodal pattern — and a remarkable finding: 41 in every 100 images score 6 or above.

Score Distribution — Full 24.000+ Image Corpus
Key Finding

41.0% of all images score ≥ 6 — classified as explicitly violent. The distribution is notably bimodal, clustering around scores 1–4 and then spiking again at 6 and 8. Score 5 forms a curious valley — suggesting the annotation model treated it as a threshold rather than a midpoint. Only 32 images reach the maximum score of 10, and 480 score 9.

Reading the Timeline

The corpus begins on February 24, 2022 — the first day of the full-scale invasion. Early months show moderate violence that rises and stabilises. Peaks correlate with major offensives and counteroffensives. The visual grammar of the war becomes increasingly entrenched over time.

Russian vs.
Ukrainian channels

Ten channels — five on each side of the conflict. The comparison yields a counterintuitive result.

Mean Violence by Channel
Mean Score by Side
Counterintuitive Finding

Ukrainian channels score higher on average (4.31 vs 4.16 for Russian channels). But this is not what it seems. The two highest-scoring channels — Донбасс Реалии and ХЕРСОН: Non Fake — are based in Russian-occupied territories. They document violence happening to their communities: strikes on civilian infrastructure, aftermath of shelling, the lived reality of occupation. Russian channels, by contrast, tend toward operational footage, weapons displays and propaganda aesthetics — which, though violent, score somewhat lower on implied violence.

What do they
post about?

Violence is not just a matter of intensity — it is also a matter of kind. The content mix differs sharply between channels, revealing fundamentally different editorial logics on each side.

Content Type Breakdown by Channel (% of posts) — RU channels above line, UA below
Russian Channels

Combat, weapons and drones dominate. rusich_army leads on combat footage (22%) and weapons (15%). voenacher and ZA_FROHT are drone-heavy. wargonzo is an outlier — nearly half its content (48%) is political, making it more of a commentary channel than a war documentation one.

Ukrainian Channels

Destruction dominates. kherson_non_fake posts destruction at 36% — more than double the corpus average. donbassrealii and hyevuy_dnepr follow at 24–25%. These occupied-territory channels document what is being done to their communities. a_shtirlitz is unique: 17% propaganda and 8% груз 200 — a channel dedicated to exposing Russian military deaths.

07 / 07

Does violence
drive reach?

With view counts for every post, we can ask: does more graphic content travel further? The answer is more nuanced than expected.

Mean Views by Violence Score
Finding

Engagement peaks at score 4 — moderate implied violence — with a mean of 220,000 views per post. Extreme violence (scores 8–9) actually receives fewer views on average, suggesting it may be shared more narrowly, filtered by platform moderation, or posted by smaller-audience channels. The exception: score 10 images average 303,000 views — but this is based on just 32 images, many of which were posted by large-audience channels like RVvoenkor (2.6M posts in corpus) and rusich_army.
