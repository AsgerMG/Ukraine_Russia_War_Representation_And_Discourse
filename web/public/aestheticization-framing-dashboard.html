Key Findings
Answering the Research Questions
🎨
As aestheticization rises, does framing rise?
Yes — the strongest relationship in the dataset. R = 0.581 and R² = 33.8%, edging out even the dehumanization → violence pairing. The climb is nearly monotonic from a=0 to a=8.
📣
Which content type is the key driver?
Propaganda memes dominate — aestheticization mean 6.64, framing mean 7.27. No other content type scores above 5.2 on framing. "ГРУZ 200" composites, flame overlays, and flag graphics are characteristic examples.
🖼️
Which frame type drives it?
Composite/meme frames (5.95 / 6.34) and professional production (4.20 / 4.05). Screenshots also score high on framing (4.29) despite lower aestheticization — maps and infographics carry ideology without visual polish.
⚡
Can framing be high without aestheticization?
Yes — screenshots show this. Tactical maps and infographics score framing ~4.3 while scoring only 3.4 on aestheticization. Ideology travels through information design, not just visual beauty.
📡
Which channel aestheticizes most?
wargonzo and a_shtirlitz lead (≈4.2–4.3). a_shtirlitz has the highest narrative framing of any channel (4.99), reflecting its heavy use of meme composites and ideological overlays. hyevuy_dnepr is the lowest on both (3.13 / 3.14).
🧮
What does the equation say?
ŷ = 1.39 + 0.69x — the low intercept (1.39) means an un-aestheticized image carries almost no framing. This is the most "intentional" pairing: polished visuals are actively crafted to carry ideology.

Cell 1 — Regression & Stats
from scipy import stats import numpy as np df_c = df[['aestheticization_score', 'narrative_framing_score']].dropna() x, y = df_c['aestheticization_score'].values, df_c['narrative_framing_score'].values slope, intercept, r, p, se = stats.linregress(x, y) print(f"ŷ = {intercept:.4f} + {slope:.4f} × aestheticization_score") print(f"R = {r:.4f} | R² = {r**2:.4f} | p = {p:.2e}")
Cell 2 — Scatter + Regression Line
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt sample = df_c.sample(3000, random_state=42) xj = sample['aestheticization_score'] + np.random.uniform(-0.3,0.3,len(sample)) yj = sample['narrative_framing_score'] + np.random.uniform(-0.3,0.3,len(sample)) fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(10,6), facecolor='#0a0e1a') ax.set_facecolor('#0f1628') ax.scatter(xj, yj, alpha=0.12, s=12, color='#005BBB') xl = np.linspace(0,10,100) ax.plot(xl, intercept+slope*xl, color='#FFD500', lw=2.5, label=f'ŷ={intercept:.2f} + {slope:.3f}x R²={r**2:.3f}') ax.tick_params(colors='#7a90b8') for sp in ax.spines.values(): sp.set_edgecolor('#1a2a4a') ax.set_xlabel('Aestheticization Score', color='#7a90b8') ax.set_ylabel('Narrative Framing Score', color='#7a90b8') ax.set_title('Aestheticization vs Narrative Framing', color='#e8eef8') ax.legend(facecolor='#0f1628', labelcolor='#e8eef8') plt.tight_layout(); plt.show()
Cell 3 — Mean Line + 95% CI Ribbon
means = df_c.groupby('aestheticization_score')['narrative_framing_score'].agg(['mean','std','count']) ci = 1.96 * means['std'] / np.sqrt(means['count']) fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(10,5), facecolor='#0a0e1a') ax.set_facecolor('#0f1628') ax.plot(means.index, means['mean'], 'o-', color='#005BBB', lw=2, ms=7) ax.fill_between(means.index, means['mean']-ci, means['mean']+ci, alpha=0.2, color='#FFD500') ax.tick_params(colors='#7a90b8') for sp in ax.spines.values(): sp.set_edgecolor('#1a2a4a') ax.set_xlabel('Aestheticization Score', color='#7a90b8') ax.set_ylabel('Mean Narrative Framing', color='#7a90b8') ax.set_title('Mean Narrative Framing by Aestheticization Level (95% CI)', color='#e8eef8') ax.set_xticks(range(11)); plt.tight_layout(); plt.show()
